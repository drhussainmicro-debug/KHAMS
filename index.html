Renal & Body Weight

Creatinine Clearance (Cockcroft–Gault)

Estimated CrCl
—
Cockcroft–Gault (SI units): [(140−age) × weight × constant] / SCr(µmol/L), constant = 1.23 (male) or 1.04 (female). Use actual body weight; consider ideal/adjusted weight if obese.

Ideal & Adjusted Body Weight

Ideal Body Weight
—
Devine formula: male 50 + 2.3 × (height in inches − 60); female 45.5 + 2.3 × (height in inches − 60). Most accurate for height ≥152 cm (5 ft).

BMI (Body Mass Index)

BMI
—
WHO bands: <18.5 underweight · 18.5–24.9 normal · 25–29.9 overweight · 30–34.9 obese I · 35–39.9 obese II · ≥40 obese III. Informs whether to use actual, ideal, or adjusted body weight for dosing.
Antibiotic Dosing

Vancomycin — surgical prophylaxis dose

Prophylaxis dose
—
1 g IV for ≤90 kg · 1.5 g IV for >90 kg. Infuse over 60–90+ min. Restricted — ID consult required past 48h.

Cefazolin — surgical prophylaxis dose

Prophylaxis dose
—
2 g IV standard · 3 g IV if >120 kg. Re-dose every 4 hours intraoperatively.

Gentamicin — prophylaxis dose by weight

Prophylaxis dose
—
Within 60 min before incision. Do not re-dose. Restricted — ID consult required past 48h.

Vancomycin — AUC₂₄ (peak/trough method)

Estimation tool only — for steady-state levels (typically 3rd–4th dose), peak drawn at end of infusion, trough drawn just before next dose. Correlate with pharmacy/ID; use validated Bayesian software where available.
Estimated AUC₂₄
—
Target 400–600 mg·hr/L for most serious MRSA infections (2020 ASHP/IDSA consensus). <400 = likely subtherapeutic; >600 = increased nephrotoxicity risk.

Colistin (Colistimethate Sodium) Dosing Restricted

Population PK method (Garonzik et al. 2011), commonly used clinically. Restricted drug — ID/microbiology consult required. Verify mg CBA ↔ IU conversion against your local product, as it varies by manufacturer.

Antimicrobial Prescribing Guidance

Core principles from the Antimicrobial Prescribing & Management Guide v1.0.0.
  1. All antibiotics listed are empirical only and should be adjusted according to cultures when final.
  2. All doses are based on normal renal function — adjust according to the patient's creatinine clearance/eGFR (see Calculators).
  3. De-escalation is imperative within 48–72 hours of starting antibiotics whenever possible.
  4. Consult ID/microbiology if needed.
  5. Any patient transferred from another hospital should be screened for MRSA (nasal + groin) and MDRO.
  6. Any patient receiving an implant should be screened for MRSA (nasal + groin) and treated with vancomycin for surgical pre-op prophylaxis.
  7. The Antibiotic Stewardship team makes regular stewardship rounds on all inpatients to confirm this policy is being adhered to, and checks cultures as needed.
  8. It is imperative to give the shortest course possible and switch IV to oral as soon as possible.
  9. The antibiotics below are restricted to the first 48 hours — if the patient still needs the antibiotic, an ID/microbiology consult is required for continued use. Other antibiotics may be added as necessary.
