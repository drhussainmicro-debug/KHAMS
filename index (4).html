Calculators

Quick bedside calculations referenced throughout the guide.

Creatinine Clearance (Cockcroft–Gault)

Estimated CrCl
—
Cockcroft–Gault: [(140−age) × weight × (0.85 if female)] / (72 × SCr). Use actual body weight; consider ideal/adjusted weight if obese.

Vancomycin — surgical prophylaxis dose

Prophylaxis dose
—
1 g IV for ≤90 kg · 1.5 g IV for >90 kg. Infuse over 60–90+ min. Restricted — ID consult required past 48h.

Cefazolin — surgical prophylaxis dose

Prophylaxis dose
—
2 g IV standard · 3 g IV if >120 kg. Re-dose every 4 hours intraoperatively.

Gentamicin — prophylaxis dose by weight

Prophylaxis dose
—
Within 60 min before incision. Do not re-dose. Restricted — ID consult required past 48h.

Stewardship Policy

Core rules from the Antimicrobial Prescribing & Management Guide v1.0.0.
✓ De-escalate within 48–72 hours whenever possible
✓ Switch IV → PO as soon as clinically able
✓ Prescribe the shortest effective course
✓ All regimens are empirical — adjust to final cultures
⚠ Restricted — 48-hour rule
GentamicinAmikacinMeropenemErtapenem (MOH)VancomycinColistin (MOH)
If the patient still needs the antibiotic after the first 48 hours, an ID/microbiology consult is required to continue use.

Screening requirements

Transferred from another hospitalScreen for MRSA (nasal + groin) and MDRO
Any patient receiving an implantScreen for MRSA (nasal + groin); treat with vancomycin for surgical pre-op prophylaxis

Stewardship oversight

The Antibiotic Stewardship team performs regular rounds on all inpatients to confirm policy adherence and reviews cultures as needed. Consult ID/microbiology whenever the clinical picture is unclear or a restricted agent is still required beyond 48 hours.

About this app

Independent, no-cost point-of-care companion to the Kuwait Hospital Antimicrobial Prescribing & Management Guide v1.0.0. Installable, works offline once loaded. Currently populated: Respiratory Tract Infections, Skin & Soft Tissue, Gastrointestinal, and Genitourinary infections, plus surgical-prophylaxis dosing tables. Remaining sections are being added incrementally.

Does not replace clinical judgement, the full written policy, or an ID/microbiology consult.
